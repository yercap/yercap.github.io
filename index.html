Pioneers of Computer Science

Two important figures in the field of computer sciecence and mathematics, Charles Babbage and Ada Lovelace are credited with large progression regarding in computational machine language and computer development. Their works and contributions in the world of computer science have paved the way for the modern future of technology we witness today. This unusual pairing of these two Victorian innovators was inspiring; Ada, the first computer programmer, and Charles, an inventor, made visions of a mechanical thing into a reality.

Charles Babbage

Charles Babbage was an English mathematician and creator born in London in 1791. His original intention was to introduce English mathematics and developments to the rest of the Europe. During the mid-1800s, Babbage began planning and creating mathematical tables such as calcuators and other computational devices. After he recieved support from the government for such machines that could do such ideas, he developed the Analytical Engine that was a prototype computer and a pioneering machine that would precede the modern computers. This was a massively innovative progresion that would establish the systems and computers that we use today.

Ada Lovelace

Augusta Ada Bryone, or Lovelace's original birthname, was the daughter of a famous poet. While her early years as a child were plagued with threats of family separation, she discovered many of her individual interests through her growing years. She is creditted with being the first computer programmer After a brief stunt in growth, she met Charles Babbage. Introducing many computer concepts was an invaluable resource for Charles Babbage's work on the Analytical Engine, and Lovelace was a frequent tester of the protoypes he procured.
